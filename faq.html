Frequently Asked Questions



Q1 What is "Meal Makers: From Waste to Taste"?
A1 Meal Makers: From Waste to Taste" is a charitable initiative that aims to reduce food waste by collecting surplus food from events such as parties, weddings, and hotels, and redistributing it to those in need..


Q2 How does "Meal Makers: From Waste to Taste" work ?
A2We collaborate with event organizers, catering services, and hospitality businesses to collect excess food that would otherwise go to waste. We then coordinate with local shelters, soup kitchens, and charitable organizations to distribute the food to individuals and families facing food insecurity..


Q3 Is the collected food safe to eat??
A3 Yes, we adhere to strict food safety guidelines and regulations to ensure that all donated food is safe for consumption. Our team members are trained in proper handling and transportation procedures to maintain food quality and safety at all times..


Q4 How can individuals or businesses get involved with "Meal Makers: From Waste to Taste"??
A4 There are several ways to get involved, including volunteering to help collect and distribute food, donating funds or resources to support our operations, or simply spreading awareness about our initiative within your community. Interested parties can contact us through our website or social media channels to learn more..


Q5 What makes "MealMakers" different from a food bank?
A5 "MealMakers" redistributes only fresh, wholesome foods, which it delivers on the day it is received. Food banks typically collect, warehouse and distribute canned and non-perishable foods. Unlike some food banks, "MealMakers" never charges a fee to the recipient agencies for the food it delivers.


Q6 If it is good food, why isn't it consumed?
A6 Overbuying, incorrect anticipation of consumer choices, manufacturing and distribution issues and weather conditions can create food overages.

Q8 What is food insecurity?
A8 Limited or uncertain availability of nutritionally adequate food.


Q9 Who is hungry?
A9 Sadly, 14 in 100 Indians does not have enough food to eat and requires emergency food assistance. And, 1 in 3 children is born into food insecure households. Women in food insecure households frequently are at the greatest risk, depriving themselves of nutritionally adequate foods to make those foods available to their children and husbands.
